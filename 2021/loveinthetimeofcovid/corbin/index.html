Pregnancy During The Pandemic

By Ryan Corbin

COVID-19 has reshaped many facets of our lives profoundly, from how we work and do grocery shopping to the way we meet with our family and friends. Binge watching has replaced friends gathering. Online classes and meetings have become the new normal. But for some people, the changes are way beyond that. The scale of the pandemic has been extended to an even more personal decision in their lives, getting pregnant. Below are the stories of three real women who have experienced firsthand the difficulties COVID-19 has presented pregnant women during the pandemic.

Michelle Palage

Michelle is a 30 year old mother of two who in October of 2020 gave birth to her second child AJ. AJ is her second child, a younger sibling to Aubrey who was born in pre-pandemic times. More information from her interview will be provided...

Common Uncertainty

Posts on r/Pregnant, a Reddit forum for people to share their experiences of pregnancy, showing that they are not alone in experiencing overhwhelming anxiety and concern for the health of their child and themselves. Using a dictionary researchers developed to rate words for positivity and negativity, we examined the average sentiment of the 44,000 posts from Jan. 2020 to Jan 2021.

Abortion During COVID-19

The mood on r/Pregnant drastically turned negative in mid-March, when COVID-19 cases started rising in the U.S. "COVID-19," "stay home," and "social distance" topped the most used words suggesting concerns about the pandemic.

Some Decided To Wait

Data about delaying pregnancy The Brookings Institute estimates that as many as 300,000 fewer children could be born in 2021. Overall, one in three U.S. women said they wanted to get pregnant later or have fewer children because of COVID-19, according to a Guttmacher Institute study.
